# have to (mv)

/ˈhæv tə/ or /ˈhæf tə/

## &bull; used to [show](../s/show-v.md#•-to-give-particular-information-or-a-time-or-measurement) that you must do [something](../s/something-p.md#•-a-thing-that-is-not-known-or-mentioned-by-name)

## &bull; used to give advice or recommend [something](../s/something-p.md#•-a-thing-that-is-not-known-or-mentioned-by-name)

## &bull; used to say that [something](../s/something-p.md#•-a-thing-that-is-not-known-or-mentioned-by-name) must be [true](../t/true-adj.md#•-connected-with-facts-rather-than-things-that-have-been-invented-or-guessed) or must [happen](../h/happen-v.md#•-to-take-place-especially-without-being-planned)

## &bull; used to suggest that an annoying [event](../e/event-n.md#•-a-thing-that-happens-especially-something-important) [happens](../h/happen-v.md#•-to-take-place-especially-without-being-planned) in order to annoy you, or that [somebody](../s/somebody-p.md#•-a-person-who-is-not-known-or-mentioned-by-name) does [something](../s/something-p.md#•-a-thing-that-is-not-known-or-mentioned-by-name) in order to annoy you
