# assumption (n)

/əˈsʌmpʃn/

## &bull; a belief or [feeling](../f/feeling-n.md#•-a-persons-emotions-rather-than-their-thoughts-or-ideas) that [something](../s/something-p.md#•-a-thing-that-is-not-known-or-mentioned-by-name) is [true](../t/true-adj.md#•-connected-with-facts-rather-than-things-that-have-been-invented-or-guessed) or that [something](../s/something-p.md#•-a-thing-that-is-not-known-or-mentioned-by-name) will [happen](../h/happen-v.md#•-to-take-place-especially-without-being-planned), although there is no [proof](../p/proof-n.md#•-information-documents-etc-that-show-that-something-is-true)

## &bull; (assumption of something) the act of taking or beginning to [have](../h/have-v.md#•-have-something-to-own-hold-or-posses-something) power or responsibility
